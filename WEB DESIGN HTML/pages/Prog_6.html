Comparative Study on Different Types of Computers

INTRODUCTION


A computer is an electronic device capable of manipulating information and data. It can store, retrieve, and process data, enabling users to perform various tasks. These tasks include creating documents, sending emails, playing games, browsing the web, and editing or creating spreadsheets, presentations, and videos.

HARDWARE

refers to any physical component of a computer, such as the keyboard, mouse, or internal parts shown in the image below

SOFTWARE

is a collection of instructions that dictate to the hardware what actions to perform and how to execute them. Web browsers, games, and word processors are all examples of software.

Every interaction you have with your computer relies on both hardware and software. For instance, you are likely currently using a web browser (software) to view this lesson and your mouse (hardware) to navigate between pages. As you delve into different computer types, consider the variations in their hardware. You will discover throughout this tutorial that diverse computer types frequently utilize distinct types of software as well.

SUPER COMPUTER

Supercomputer of any kind is an extremely high-functioning type of computer whose system is available at any given time. Common uses of supercomputers are for high mathematical computation and research. Examples of its application are for predicting the weather patterns, nuclear creation, mathematical models, and cryptology. The price for the supercomputers has significantly declined, and many business have utilized

MAINFRANE COMPUTER

Mainframe computers are known to be the “big iron” that operates on a larger scale and processes huge amounts of data. It may be smaller that the supercomputer, but the mainframe computer's aspects are excellent at high reliability, massive data processing, security, scalability, asvirtualization, compatibility, longevity, and cost-effectiveness.

MINI COMPUTER

Mini computers are computers sitting in between mainframe computers and microcomputers. Minicomputers are used often in low- to mid-size businesses, research, and managing files and data, as well as educational computers. Computers such as this are favored amongst the general office and educational workforces for their robust capability, speed, cost, and size.

SERVER

A server is a powerful machine that computes and stores data and information throughout the internet. A personal computer and any other computer can be used as a personal server, which can act as a server that taps into the internet to collaborate and open websites. Servers are capable of more than just basic computer functions. They manage intricate server processes, including handling numerous user queries per second, hosting content-rich websites, establishing shared drives for networked devices, and processing demanding workloads like database transaction management, which requires substantial computing power.

WORKSTATION

A high-performance computer designed for demanding professional tasks like 3D modeling, scientific research, or heavy video editing. Think of it as a regular PC, but much more powerful.

MICROCOMPUTER

An older term for what we now just call a personal computer (PC)—like a desktop or laptop. It was named "micro" because its main processor (CPU) is a single microprocessor chip.
